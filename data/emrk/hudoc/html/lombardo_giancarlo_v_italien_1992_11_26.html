Information Note on the Court’s case-law No.

November 1992

Giancarlo Lombardo v. Italy - 12490/86

Judgment 26.11.1992

Article 6

Article 6-1

Reasonable time

Length of civil proceedings in Court of Audit: violation

[This summary is extracted from the Court’s official reports (Series A or Reports of Judgments and Decisions). Its formatting and structure may therefore differ from the Case-Law Information Note summaries.]

I. ARTICLE 6 § 1 OF THE CONVENTION ("REASONABLE TIME")

A. Applicability

Obligation on State to adjust pension paid to former judge in accordance with legislation in force:  State not using discretionary powers - comparable to employer party to contract of employment governed by private law - right in issue a civil right.

Conclusion: applicable (unanimously).

B. Compliance

1. Period to be taken into consideration

Starting-point: institution of proceedings in Court of Audit.

End: filing of judgment.

Total duration: approximately eight years and four months, including two sets of proceedings in the Constitutional Court.

2. Applicable criteria

Reasonableness of length of proceedings: to be assessed with reference to criteria laid down in Court's case-law and in light of circumstances of case, which in this instance called for an overall assessment.

Complexity of case not in itself justifying length of proceedings - delays so substantial that overall length of proceedings excessive.

Conclusion: violation (unanimously).

II. ARTICLE 50 OF THE CONVENTION

Non-pecuniary damage sufficiently compensated by finding of violation (unanimously).
